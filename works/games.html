Games

Create interactive entertainment that is playful and shareable

Situation

BuzzFeed content creators wanted to experiment with simple game formats that are fun and shareable. After the breakout success of the quiz format, our editors are looking for more playful ways to connect with the audiences.

Solution

Create a collection of turn-key game formats for mobile and desktop that is easy to make and adapt for different content.

Along three engineers, a producer, and an illustrator who also have experienced in making games, I led the UX design and collaborated with editors to explore a wide range of light-weight game formats. Over the course of 12 months, we prototyped 15 turn-key formats and published over 60 games. For the most popular games, we also created a backend game maker tool that allows content creators to generate their own games. Here are some of my favorite formats:

Memory Game

As a coffee shop barista, the player has to remember a series of complex coffee orders and tap the ingredients in the right sequence. How Long Would You Last As A Barista? got over 380k views.

Image Mixer

The player creates a shareable, Pinterest friendly composition by mixing and matching multiple images. The prototype game Can You Pick A Hipster Outfit For Snow White? got over 500k+ views.

The player chooses the desired options with a slider to generate fun, unexpected results.

20-questions

Based on a classic 20-questions game, Emoji Oracle asks a series of binary yes/no question to guess the emoji you have in mind. Try it yourself!

The player controls the avatar to avoid falling objects by tapping the left and right side of the screen. The games are fully customizable to adjust a different theme from graphic assets, animation speed, and score. Try it yourself

Results

With an average of 30-40% completion rate, many games went on becomes a huge hit. Although our audiences, as well as our editors, love the game products, each project required too much effort to keep up the incoming demands. On one end, our team struggled to keep the games performant on mobile and desktop, as well as shareable across the social platforms. On another end, our content creators are frustrated that they couldn't experiment ideas on their own at a faster pace.

After 12 months of experimentation phase, the strategy was shifted to scale up a few successful formats by integrating them into our in-house content management system. The game team got dissolved and some of the OG team members joined the bigger CMS team to incorporate those popular formats as part of the publishing workflow.

Role: Product design. Team: 4 engineers, 1 producer and 1 illustrator.
