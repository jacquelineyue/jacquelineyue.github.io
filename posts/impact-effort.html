Impact ✕ Effort Framework

Visualize and measure impact and effort for better prioritization

Sequencing the projects for a cross-disciplined team is a constant challenge. As part of a team that builds internal analytic tools, our projects often have multiple dependencies among ourselves and with other teams that are crisscross throughout the life cycle of product development:

    Questions that came up often during our roadmap process
  • What is the logical sequence of the projects?
  • How might we prioritize projects that are the most impactful to our users?
  • The external backend team is our biggest dependency that often has limited bandwidth. Is there any task that requires no backend work we can start on to keep the project going forward?
  • What are the quick-wins projects we can bang out quickly, so the users feel the continuous improvement of the product and our team feels the ongoing impact of their works?

Outcomes

  • Transparency is the biggest win. After the putting in the estimate, it is very clear and easy to compare project by project, which project we should bet on, which project we can deprioritize. This also leaves no room for low-impact but laborious project, and hard to get our team advocate for it.
  • An unintended outcome: The spreadsheet highlights our gap for staffing, so we can show what other projects we could have invested if we have more people.
  • The Impact x Effort idea has inspired other teams across the BuzzFeed tech to approach project prioritization similarly and adapted the spreadsheet.
