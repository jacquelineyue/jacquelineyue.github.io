Product & Design

Engage the BuzzFeed audience with a variety of game formats that are simple to make

BuzzFeed content creators want to experiment ideas with simple, interactive formats that are engaging, shareable and fun. After the breakout success of the quiz format, our editors are looking for more playful ways to connect with the readers.

The goal is to design and build a collection of simple, turn-key action games for our editorial team that were super easy to create and adapt.

Along three engineers, a producer, and an illustrator, I led the design and collaborated with editors to explore a wide range of light-weight game formats. Over the course of 9 months, we developed 15 turn-key formats and prototyped over 60 game ideas. For the most popular games, we also created a backend game maker tool that allows content creators to generate their own games.

Type-in Trivia

As one of the most requested game formats, we develop a type-in trivia and a simple CMS that allows our content creators to quickly create a trivia game by uploading the images and writing the question-answer text.

3

Name Generator

The player inputs their name to generate a custom artifact that was inspired by the TV shows "Orange is the New Black".

4

Memory Game

As a coffee shop's barista, the player has to remember a series of complex coffee orders from different customers and tap the ingredients in the right sequence. The final game How Long Would You Last As A Barista? got over 380k pageviews.

5

Image Mixer

The player creates a shareable, Pinterest friendly composition by mixing and matching multiple images. The prototype game Can You Pick A Hipster Outfit For Snow White? got over 500k+ pageviews.

Results

With an average of 35-40% completion rate, many games went on becomes a huge hit. This validated that game format is a worthy investment to keep the BuzzFeed audiences engaged and entertained.

After a 9-months experimentation phase, the product strategy had shifted to scale up the game output by developing a more robust game-making tool. The game team got dissolved as some of the OG team joined the bigger Content Managment System team to integrate the popular formats as part of the publishing workflow.

Role: Product design. Team: 4 engineers, 1 producer and 1 illustrator.
