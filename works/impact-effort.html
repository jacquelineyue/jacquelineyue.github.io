Impact ✕ Effort Worksheet

Visualize impact and effort for better prioritization

Sequencing projects for a cross-disciplined team is a constant challenge. As part of a team that builds internal analytic tools, our projects often have multiple dependencies among ourselves or with other teams. Here is a life cycle of a typical project:

The problems we face during prioritization

  • What is the logical sequence of the projects?
  • How can we prioritize projects that are the most impactful to our users?
  • We have limit back-end engineers available. Is there any task that requires no back-end work we can start to keep the project going forward?
  • What are the quick-wins projects we can bang out quickly, so the users feel the continuous improvement of the product and our team feels the ongoing impact of their works?

What would be the best way to evaluate a bunch of ideas simultaneously?

Trading card game, each idea is given a score for its core values, which would allow us to compare the stats against each other, rank their importance, and determine which one to bet on.

Outcomes

There are many roadmap methodologies on product management out there, but this simple Impact x Effort worksheet works for my team for a few reasons:

  • Our users are also our co-workers. We know more visibility the internal user goals compared to external consumers, therefore more precise knowledge about the impact of our product.
  • Transparency is the biggest win for us. For our team, the spreadsheet shows everyone why we purpuse certain projects and deprioritize others. For our management, the spreadsheet highlights our staffing needs by having the list of projects the team could have worked on if we have the more people.
